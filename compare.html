Formula 1

Cars & Racing Style

F1 is a high-speed open-top car race which usees 1.6L V6 engine cars.

Despite having less engine capacity, F1 cars are more powerful, capable of generating around 1000 BHP. Both

Each F1 car is uniquely engineered to suit the individual drivers and typically undergo more development by each team. F1 cars are often very different from one another in both engineering and performance.

Teams

The F1 teams are: Alfa Romeo, AlphaTauri, Aston Martin, Ferrari, Haas, McLaren, Mercedes, Red Bull, and Williams.

Tracks & Races

F1 tracks all have different shapes, with designated tracks and street circuits. These tracks typically have to be of higher grade than those of Indycar. F1 races are international, covering the 5 continents while Indycar races mostly occur in the United States.

Indycar

Cars & Racing Style

Like F1, Indycar is also a high-speed open-top car race that uses V6 engines in their cars. However, Indycar engines are slightly larger at 2.2 Liters.

Indycar vehicles are able to push out around 700 BHP. F1 and Indycar are moving towards 100% renewable fuel sources.

Car engineering is a less important factor in Indycar, with each team and car running the same chassis. Each car is more similar than different, which means the field is more even on a car performance level.

Teams

The teams in Indycar are: A.J. Foyt Enterprises, Andretti Autosport, Arrow Mclaren SP, Chip Ganassi Racing, Dale Coyne Racing, Ed Carpenter Racing, Juncos Hollinger Racing, Meyer Shank Racing, Rahal Letterman Lanigan Racing, Team Penske.

Tracks & Races

Indycar races typically occur on street circuits or ovals in speedways.
